Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

(1931-2015)

              Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, also known as the Missile Man of India, was the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, he was a scientist and an engineer who worked as an Aerospace engineer with the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
He played a key role in India's civilian space programme and military missile development. He was also known for his motivational speeches and books that inspired millions of young people in India and around the world. Abdul Kalam died on 27 July 2015 in Shillong, Meghalaya, India, while delivering a lecture. He will always be remembered as a great leader, scientist, and above all, a teacher.
